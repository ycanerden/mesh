# Product Marketing Context — Habitat Residency

*Last updated: March 12, 2026*

---

## Product Overview

**One-liner:**
A 3-month in-person residency for solo founders to build, launch, and scale their startups — together.

**What it does:**
Habitat Residency brings together ambitious solo founders to transform from idea to traction in an intensive 12-week program. You get structured frameworks (the proven 5-hour sprint methodology), peer accountability, expert mentorship, and the Habitat Skills AI co-founder toolkit — all designed to help you ship fast and find your first 100 users.

**Product category:**
Startup accelerator / Founder residency program

**Product type:**
In-person cohort-based program with ongoing community access

**Business model:**
- Application-based admission (selective)
- Equity stake or program fee (TBD based on funding model)
- Alumni network and ongoing community benefits
- Potential follow-on investment opportunities

## Target Audience

**Target founders:**
- Solo founders (or very early 2-person teams)
- Pre-seed to early-stage (idea to pre-revenue or early traction)
- Technical or technical-adjacent (comfortable using AI tools)
- Europe-based or willing to relocate for 3 months
- Age-agnostic but typically 25-45

**Decision-makers:**
The founder themselves (individual decision, not committee)

**Primary use case:**
Going from validated idea to launched product with first paying customers

**Jobs to be done:**
- Get unblocked on building and shipping (overcome analysis paralysis)
- Find accountability and momentum (escape solo founder isolation)
- Learn proven frameworks for validation, building, and marketing (avoid costly mistakes)
- Build alongside peers who understand the journey (community over competition)

**Specific use cases:**
- Technical founder with an idea but no marketing/GTM experience
- Non-technical founder using AI tools (Claude Code, Cursor) to build
- Founder stuck in the "building in isolation" trap
- Solo founder who tried accelerators but felt overlooked compared to teams
- Immigrant/international founder building in Europe without a strong local network

## Problems & Pain Points

**Core problem:**
Solo founders are building in isolation, without accountability, frameworks, or community — leading to months of wasted effort, abandoned projects, and burnout.

**Why alternatives fall short:**
- **Traditional accelerators (YC, Techstars):** Built for teams, not solos. Solo founders feel like second-class citizens. Generic advice doesn't fit the solo path.
- **Online courses/communities:** No accountability, easy to ghost, lacks in-person energy and momentum
- **Coworking spaces:** Space but no structure, community but no shared mission
- **Building alone:** Complete freedom but crushing isolation, no one to reality-check ideas, easy to give up

**What it costs them:**
- **Time:** 6-12 months building the wrong thing with no users
- **Money:** Wasted on features nobody wants, tools they don't need
- **Opportunity:** Missing the 2025-2026 AI-native founder wave
- **Emotional:** Burnout, imposter syndrome, giving up on dreams

**Emotional tension:**
"I know I should just ship something, but I'm scared it's the wrong thing. Everyone says solo founding is harder, and I'm starting to believe them. What if I'm wasting my time?"

## Competitive Landscape

**Direct competitors:**
- **Solo Founders Program (solofounders.com):** 3-month SF-based residency for solos. Falls short because: US-only (visa challenges for Europeans), no technical/AI focus, less structured methodology
- **On Deck Founders (beondeck.com):** Online fellowship for early founders. Falls short because: online-only (no in-person intensity), generalist (not solo-specific), more networking than building
- **Antler:** Pre-idea accelerator that matches co-founders. Falls short because: forces team formation (we celebrate solo), earlier stage (pre-idea), less community-driven

**Secondary competitors:**
- **YC / Techstars / 500 Global:** Traditional accelerators. Fall short because: built for teams, not solo-friendly, batch sizes dilute attention, generic playbooks
- **Indie Hackers / Hacker News communities:** Online communities. Fall short because: no structure, no accountability, async vs in-person energy

**Indirect competitors:**
- **Building alone:** Complete autonomy, no cost. Falls short because: isolation, no frameworks, high failure rate, easy to quit
- **Getting a co-founder:** More hands, shared burden. Falls short because: hard to find the right person, equity complications, relationship risk

## Differentiation

**Key differentiators:**
1. **Solo-first philosophy:** We don't just tolerate solo founders — we celebrate them. Solo is the future.
2. **5-hour sprint methodology:** Proven framework from 101+ prototypes launched. Ship in one evening, not one quarter.
3. **AI-native:** Built for the Claude Code / Cursor generation. Habitat Skills = your technical co-founder.
4. **In-person intensity:** 3 months together in Europe, not Zoom calls. Energy, serendipity, momentum.
5. **Community over competition:** "Solo, together." Peer support, not VC beauty pageants.

**How we solve it differently:**
Traditional accelerators optimize for VC fundraising. We optimize for **shipping and traction**. You leave with a launched product, real users, and revenue (or validated learnings).

**Why that's better:**
Fundraising is easier when you already have traction. And most founders don't need VC — they need customers. We help you get both.

**Why customers choose us:**
- "I tried YC — felt like a second-class citizen as a solo founder. Habitat gets it."
- "Other programs were all talk. Habitat made me ship in week 1."
- "The AI-native toolkit (Skills) is like having a co-founder who never sleeps."
- "I wasn't alone anymore. The cohort became my board of advisors."

## Objections

| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| **"Isn't solo founding just... harder?"** | Yes — if you do it alone. But with the right frameworks, peer support, and AI tools, you move faster than most teams. 1/3 of new startups are solo-founded (Carta data). The future is solo. |
| **"I don't want to give up equity"** | Fair. We're still finalizing the model. Some residencies charge a fee, some take small equity (~2-5%), some are free with follow-on investment. What matters: is 3 months of focused building + community worth it? |
| **"I can't take 3 months off"** | You're right — this isn't for everyone. But if your startup is a side project forever, it'll stay a side project. The founders who win go all-in. Can you afford NOT to? |
| **"What if I don't get accepted?"** | We're selective, yes. But apply anyway. Worst case: you don't get in. Best case: your life changes. The application itself is a validation exercise. |

**Anti-persona:**
- Founders looking for VC connections over customers (we help with traction first, fundraising second)
- Teams of 3+ (this is for solos and tiny teams, not growth-stage startups)
- People wanting "advice" but not willing to ship and iterate (we're builders, not philosophers)
- Founders not willing to relocate for 3 months (this is in-person or nothing)

## Notes & Decisions to Make

**Open questions for founder:**
1. **Equity vs fee model?** What's the business model? (Solo Founders takes equity, others charge)
2. **Cohort size?** 12? 20? 30? (Smaller = tighter community, larger = economies of scale)
3. **Location?** Which European city? (Amsterdam? Berlin? Lisbon? Paris?)
4. **Duration?** Confirmed 3 months or flexible? (Some do 12 weeks, some do 6 months)
5. **Funding?** Bootstrapped? Sponsored? Grant-funded? (Affects pricing model)
6. **Application timeline?** When do apps open? When does first cohort start?

**Assumptions to validate:**
- Solo founders will relocate for 3 months (vs prefer remote/hybrid)
- Europe-based demand is sufficient (vs better to launch in SF/US)
- In-person > online (vs online has larger TAM)
- AI-native positioning resonates (vs alienates non-technical founders)
