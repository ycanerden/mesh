Cold calling

[Slide 19]
What are they?
Cold calling is the solicitation of a potential customer who had no prior interaction with a salesperson.
Warm calling, on the other hand, is the solicitation of a customer who had previously expressed interest in the company or product. 

Why are they difficult?
Not wired to behave this way.
Anxiety and fear of rejection.
People are overwhelmed.
Better marketing approaches.
Filters and spam detectors.
Cold Calls
What do you do when you get a cold call???

[Slide 20]
Start with a good call list
Take the time you need to create a targeted and high value list of prospects
Use a systematic process to create your target list 

Research is valuable…up-to-a-point
Profile your prospects and categorize them.
Leverage commonalities 
One connection point is enough

Now is not the time to sell your product or service
Focus on the prospects wants/needs and sell the value of a conversation
Few people have time or interest on the initial contact
Fundamental Rules

[Slide 21]
Take ‘No’ for an answer
Target total number of appointments, not individual yes/no
Understand the primary objections
Time, interest, cost, decision maker 

Scrap the script.  
Be situational, flexible and conversational
Listen and respond accordingly
This is not a data dump 

Minimize expectations – maximize impact.
Not a sales call
Set time expectations
Present insight and value
Fundamental Rules

[Slide 22]
Tips and techniques
Focus all of your questions on your client, not yourself
 Plan all of your questions in advance
 Don’t follow a tight cold calling scripts
 Don’t overwhelm your prospect during the first meeting
 Don’t attempt to sell on your first cold call
 Keep your prospect relaxed
 Find out what benefit will make your prospect buy from you

Opportunity insight: Selling a particular idea or strategy a buyer should pursue but might not know about (Knowledge Broker)

Interaction insight: Providing value in the form of creating insights through buyer and seller conversation (Sales Process)
