# Market Insight Report: Ghent March 2026 Cohort
## A Visionary’s Perspective (Steve Jobs Mode)

### 1. Simon Mestdagh — Tokora
**The Problem:** The invisible wall of mistrust. CEOs don’t want governance; they want to know their agents won't burn the house down.
**Jobs-ian Insight:** Reframing governance as the **"Permission to Act."**
**The Insanely Great Move:** A **"Trust Dashboard"** for the C-Suite. It shouldn't be about rules; it should be about a visual peace of mind. Let them see the trust boundaries in real-time.
**First 100 Strategy:** Find the people who *think different* in regulated industries. The ones who *want* to use AI but are tied by chains of compliance. Give them the key.

### 2. Jorel Tchana — Suniter
**The Problem:** The distraction of technology. Efficiency is a trap if it takes you away from the soul of the work.
**Jobs-ian Insight:** The **"Soul of Hospitality."** The real problem isn't restaurant efficiency; it's the chef being a data-entry clerk instead of a creator.
**The Insanely Great Move:** The **"Invisible POS."** Technology that works so well you forget it’s there. A system that listens, acts, and gets out of the way so the chef can look a customer in the eye.
**First 100 Strategy:** The independent local bistros. The ones where the chef *is* the brand. Show them they can have a world-class operation without losing their soul.

### 3. Fanny Kilanga — Career Guidance
**The Problem:** The loss of identity. People aren't looking for a "job"; they're looking for a mirror that shows them who they *actually* are.
**Jobs-ian Insight:** **"Identity Reclamation."** Most tech roles are boxes. People want to be the whole shape.
**The Insanely Great Move:** A **"Play Personality Pulse."** A tool that maps your inherent curiosity and play style to a role where you aren't just an "employee," but a visionary contributor.
**First 100 Strategy:** The "High-Achiever Burnouts." The people who have done everything "right" but feel empty. They will pay anything to find their "Why."

### 4. Christine Duong — Nitrogen Reporting
**The Problem:** The friction of bureaucracy. A reporting tool isn't a tool; it's a key to a cell.
**Jobs-ian Insight:** The **"Freedom to Build."** Architects want to build, not report. Every minute spent on a spreadsheet is a minute stolen from a design.
**The Insanely Great Move:** A real-time **"Compliance Blueprint"** plugin. As you draw the line in your CAD tool, the nitrogen impact is calculated and reported invisibly. The architect never leaves their flow.
**First 100 Strategy:** The avant-garde architects in Flanders who are being stalled by new regulations. They are desperate to build something beautiful and will adopt any tool that clears the path.
